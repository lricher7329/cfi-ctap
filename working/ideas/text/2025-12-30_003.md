# Idea

Integrate research security concepts throughout the CFI CTAP project, positioning the TRE and centralized biobanking infrastructure as critical national security safeguardsâ€”not just research tools. Health data is now classified as "dual-use" (valuable for both research and national security), requiring compliance with federal STRAC/NSGRP frameworks.

## Context

The research memo at `ideas/research/research_security.md` identified that:

1. **Health data is dual-use**: Aggregated health datasets are essential for training AI models; foreign adversaries could use Canadian health data for competitive biopharma AI or population vulnerability analysis
2. **CTAP uses Sensitive Technology Research Areas (STRA)**: AI, advanced computing, and large-scale analytics place this work under STRAC oversight
3. **TRE is a compliance engine**: The three-zone architecture automatically enforces STRAC (prevent knowledge transfer) and HIA (least privilege, data sovereignty)
4. **Centralized biobanking reduces risk**: Distributed specimen storage is vulnerable to sample loss, unauthorized access, and diversion
